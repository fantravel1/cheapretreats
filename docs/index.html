Clear labeling

Healing without spiritual pressure

One of the biggest barriers to retreat-going is not knowing if you’ll be asked to chant, pray, hold crystals, or “open your third eye.” Some people love that. Others need it clearly labeled.

Every retreat on CheapRetreats is tagged so you know exactly what you’re walking into — before you pack your bag.

Secular

No spiritual framework. Evidence-based or simply restful. No prayers, no rituals, no energy talk.

Spiritual-Optional

Spiritual elements are available but never required. Skip the meditation, keep the garden walks.

Religious

Rooted in a faith tradition. Clearly labeled so you know what you're walking into.

Trauma-Informed

Facilitators trained in trauma response. Opt-out policies. No surprise breathwork or forced vulnerability.

Neurodivergent-Friendly

Sensory accommodations, flexible scheduling, quiet spaces, and facilitators who actually understand what that means.

Why does this matter? Because a person processing trauma doesn’t need surprise group breathwork. Because an atheist grieving their partner deserves rest without being told it’s “part of God’s plan.” Because neurodivergent people deserve spaces designed with them in mind, not as an afterthought.

Can’t afford a retreat?

We’re a bridge, not a gatekeeper. Many retreats offer ways to attend for less — or free.

We believe that if you need rest, you shouldn’t have to prove you deserve it first. These options exist because enough people agree.

Sliding Scale

Pay what you can. Many retreats adjust pricing based on your situation — no paperwork, no shame, just honesty.

Scholarships

Full or partial funding from retreat centers and community sponsors. We list which ones have open applications right now.

Work Exchange

Help with meals, cleaning, or gardening in exchange for your stay. Usually 2-4 hours per day, the rest is yours.

Donation-Based

Pay what feels right afterward. No minimum. No judgment. Some of the best retreats in the world run on this model.
