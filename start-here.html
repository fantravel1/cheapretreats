Step 2

What can you spend?

Total cost. Nights included. Food included. No surprises.

Under $500

Weekend resets, silent stays, donation-based retreats

Typically 2-3 nights. Shared rooms, simple meals, minimal programming. Think monastery guesthouses, rural meditation centers, and community-run spaces. Often includes meals. Best for: a quick reset when you need to step away but can't take a full week.

Under $750

3-5 night retreats with meals and nature immersion

Mid-length stays with more structure. Usually includes all meals, a private or semi-private room, and access to facilitated sessions (yoga, meditation, group walks). Often in nature settings — forest, coast, mountains. Best for: when you need enough time to actually decompress, not just catch your breath.

Under $1,000

Full-week programs with comprehensive support

5-7 nights with dedicated programming. Expect private rooms, full meal plans, daily facilitated sessions, and often one-on-one time with a practitioner. These retreats tend to focus on specific needs — grief, trauma recovery, burnout — with trained staff. Best for: when you need real, sustained support and enough time for something to shift.

I can't afford any of these

Scholarships, work exchange, sliding scale, donation-based

Many retreats offer sliding-scale pricing, work-exchange programs (4-5 hours of help per day in exchange for free stay), full scholarships, or donation-based models where you pay what you can. We list all of these. Money should not be the thing that stops you.
